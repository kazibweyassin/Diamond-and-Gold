## 1. Executive Summary

This Compliance Pack provides buyers and partners with the documentation, policies, and templates necessary to evaluate, transact, and take delivery of gold sourced and supplied by Diamond Capital Africa (DCA).

It is intended for institutional buyers, refiners, compliance officers, and logistics partners operating in Africa and internationally.

**Contents:** company credentials, KYC templates, chain of custody & origin verification, assay examples, compliance procedures (AML/sanctions), export documentation, insurance & logistics, quality control, responsible sourcing policy, pricing & settlement terms, dispute resolution, fraud prevention, certifications, and annexed templates.

DCA operates under Ugandan law and adheres to internationally recognised standards including the OECD Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Mineral Supply Chains and LBMA Responsible Gold Guidance where applicable.

## 3. KYC & Onboarding

### 3.1 Purpose

To ensure that both buyer and seller meet legal and AML requirements before any transaction is initiated. DCA will not initiate a trade with any party that has not completed and passed KYC review.

### 3.2 Required Documents — All Counterparties

**For corporate entities:**
- Certificate of incorporation
- Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO) declaration (all UBOs ≥ 10% ownership)
- Latest proof of registered address (not older than 3 months)
- Authorised signatory list and certified ID copies for each signatory
- VAT / tax registration certificate (if applicable)
- Banking details and bank reference letter from a preferred correspondent bank
- Purpose of purchase / end-use declaration (on company letterhead)

**For individuals:**
- Government-issued ID (passport preferred)
- Proof of residential address (not older than 3 months)
- Source of funds declaration
- Purpose of purchase declaration

## 4. Chain of Custody & Origin Verification

DCA maintains documented provenance for each lot sourced from point of acquisition through to delivery to the buyer.

### 4.1 Step-by-Step Process

1. **Supplier due-diligence form (signed)** — supplier identity, mine or area of origin, payment history, and local permit verification.
2. **Lot registration** — a unique lot ID (format: DCA-LOT-YYYY-NNN) is assigned and logged in DCA's internal register at the point of procurement.
3. **Custody transfer receipts** — signed handoff documents completed at every custody point: supplier → DCA procurement → secure holding → assay lab → packing → logistics.
4. **Assay sampling** — samples collected under chain-of-custody seals with unique sample identifiers prior to dispatch to the assay lab.
5. **Packing list and custody manifest** — accompany every dispatch.
6. **Photographic record** — photographs taken at packing stage and retained for minimum 7 years.

### 4.2 Supplier Due-Diligence Checklist

```

```

### 4.3 High-Risk Origin Protocol

Where origin is from a region flagged as elevated-risk (e.g. eastern DRC conflict-affected zones), DCA applies additional controls:
- Independent third-party site visit report (where feasible)
- Cross-referencing with IPIS or PRADD mine databases
- Escalation to senior management for approval before procurement

## 5. Assay & Testing

### 5.1 Assaying Process Summary

All gold lots sourced by DCA are subject to independent laboratory assay prior to offering to buyers. Samples are collected under chain-of-custody controls and dispatched to laboratories holding ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation. Results are reported on signed lab certificates identifying the lot, method, purity, and analyst.

### 5.2 Accepted Assay Methods

| Method | Typical Accuracy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Fire assay (gravimetric) | ±0.01% | Gold standard for Au purity |
| XRF (X-ray fluorescence) | ±0.1–0.5% | Faster; screening use |
| ICP-OES | ±0.05% | Used for trace element analysis |

DCA recommends fire assay for all contract-grade determinations. XRF is used for preliminary screening only.

### 5.3 Sample Assay Report (Anonymised)

```
Assay Report
Lab:            Accredited Assay Services Ltd.
Report ID:      AR-2026-0001
Client:         Diamond Capital Africa
Lot ID:         DCA-LOT-2026-001
Method:         Fire assay (Au by gravimetric)
Result:         99.95% Au
Sample mass:    50 g
Date issued:    2026-05-01
Analyst:        [Name and signature]
Accreditation:  ISO/IEC 17025 (scope: precious metal assay — attached)
```

### 5.4 Interpretation Guidance for Buyers

- Compare reported purity against contract specifications before confirming settlement.
- Account for assay method variance: fire assay results may differ from XRF by up to 0.5%.
- DCA strongly recommends independent re-assay on receipt for:
  - First shipment from a new seller
  - Any lot above USD 100,000 in value
  - Any shipment where tamper-evident seals show signs of interference

## 6. Compliance Procedures (AML, Sanctions, Record-Keeping)

### 6.1 AML Policy Summary

DCA maintains a formal Anti-Money Laundering policy reviewed annually. Key principles:

- All counterparties are screened against OFAC, UN Consolidated List, EU Consolidated List, and INTERPOL red notices at onboarding and before each shipment.
- Transactions above defined monetary thresholds trigger Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD). Current thresholds align with Ugandan Financial Intelligence Authority (FIA) regulations.
- All trade records are archived for a minimum of **7 years**.
- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) are filed with the Uganda Financial Intelligence Authority and, where required, with relevant international bodies.
- DCA does not transact in cash for amounts above the regulatory threshold.

### 6.2 Sanctions & Screening Workflow

```
Step 1: Counterparty name/entity input into screening database
Step 2: Automated scan against OFAC, UN, EU, local FIA lists
Step 3: Results reviewed — clear / possible match / confirmed match
Step 4: Confirmed match → transaction blocked; compliance officer notified
Step 5: Possible match → manual review; EDD initiated if warranted
Step 6: Pre-shipment re-screen for all active trades
Step 7: Result logged with reviewer name and date
```

### 6.3 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Triggers

EDD is applied when any of the following are present:
- Transaction value exceeds USD 50,000 (or local currency equivalent)
- Counterparty is a PEP or close associate
- Counterparty is in a high-risk jurisdiction (FATF grey/black list)
- Transaction structure appears unusual or lacks clear commercial rationale
- Adverse media identified during screening

### 6.4 Record Retention

| Document type | Minimum retention period |
|---|---|
| KYC documents | 7 years post-relationship end |
| Transaction invoices | 7 years |
| Shipping and customs documents | 7 years |
| Assay reports | 7 years |
| Custody logs and manifests | 7 years |
| Screening records | 7 years |
| SAR filings | 7 years (restricted access) |

Records are stored securely with role-based access controls. Copies are available to auditing buyers under NDA.

### 7.2 Export Regulatory Notes

- UAE/Dubai: imports must meet DMCC Good Delivery or equivalent standards for formal exchange. DCA can supply assay-backed doré bars for refining.
- European Union: imports subject to EU Conflict Minerals Regulation (Regulation 2017/821) — DCA maintains OECD-aligned due diligence documentation to support buyers' compliance.
- All buyers are responsible for compliance with import regulations in their own jurisdiction. DCA provides documentation but does not give legal advice.

## 8. Insurance & Logistics

### 8.1 Insurance Coverage

- Shipments are insured for full replacement value from point of dispatch to delivery to buyer's nominated address.
- Insurance is arranged through reputable insurers with gold and precious metals expertise.
- All buyers receive a copy of the insurance certificate prior to dispatch.
- Buyers may request an insurance certificate naming themselves as additional insured at their own cost.

### 8.2 Claims Process

1. Notify DCA (compliance@diamondcapitalafrica.com) and the insurer within **24 hours** of loss, damage, or tamper evidence.
2. Preserve all packaging, tamper-evident seals, and shipping documents — do not dispose of any materials.
3. Submit a written incident report to DCA with photographs.
4. Cooperate with insurer's inspection and investigation.
5. DCA will co-ordinate the claim process on behalf of the insured parties.

### 8.3 Logistics Partners

All logistics partners used by DCA meet the following criteria:
- Licensed and bonded for precious metals transport
- Documented internal chain-of-custody procedures
- Insured for full replacement value
- Subject to DCA due-diligence review prior to engagement

DCA uses dedicated secure couriers and vault-to-vault transfers where applicable. Air freight is used for international shipments above a defined value threshold.

## 11. Conflict Minerals & Responsible Sourcing

### 11.1 Policy Statement

DCA is committed to responsible sourcing and will not knowingly purchase or facilitate the sale of gold that finances armed groups, involves child labour, or originates from illegally operated mines. This commitment is embedded in supplier due diligence, procurement contracts, and ongoing monitoring.

### 11.2 OECD Alignment

DCA's due diligence process follows the five-step OECD framework:

1. Establish strong company management systems (internal policy, supply chain mapping).
2. Identify and assess risks in the supply chain.
3. Design and implement a strategy to respond to identified risks.
4. Carry out independent third-party audit of supply chain due diligence (in progress; prioritised for high-volume operations).
5. Report annually on supply chain due diligence (internal; available to buyers on request).

### 11.3 Supplier Contractual Obligations

All DCA suppliers are contractually required to:
- Disclose mine or origin location and provide supporting permits.
- Confirm no use of child labour in extraction or processing.
- Confirm no sale of gold to armed groups or militias.
- Accept DCA site visits or third-party audit referrals.

Breach of these obligations is a ground for immediate contract termination and referral to relevant authorities.

## 12. Pricing & Settlement

### 12.1 Pricing Basis

Gold is priced by reference to the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) Gold Price AM or PM fix on the agreed valuation date, adjusted for:
- Purity (as determined by agreed assay method)
- Weight (troy ounces)
- Applicable discount or premium negotiated in the Sales & Purchase Agreement (SPA)

DCA provides a formal price indication sheet upon request for indicative purposes only. Binding prices are set in the executed SPA.

### 12.2 Standard Payment Terms

| Term | Description |
|---|---|
| Deposit | 30% of total contract value, payable upon SPA execution |
| Balance | 70% payable against presentation of shipping documents and assay certificate |
| Currency | USD (preferred); alternatives by agreement |
| Payment method | Bank-to-bank wire transfer only — no crypto, no cash above regulatory threshold |
| Late payment | Interest on overdue amounts at 2% per month |

DCA will not release shipments until deposit is received and cleared. Buyers are advised to engage their own legal counsel before executing any SPA.

### 12.3 Escrow Option

For first-time buyers or high-value transactions, DCA recommends transacting through a mutually agreed escrow agent. DCA can provide a list of preferred escrow providers upon request.

## 13. Dispute Resolution & Arbitration

### 13.1 Escalation Process

1. Raise the dispute in writing to compliance@diamondcapitalafrica.com with full supporting documentation.
2. DCA acknowledges receipt within 24 hours and assigns a case reference.
3. DCA proposes a resolution within 5 business days of acknowledgement.
4. If unresolved, parties enter a 15-day good-faith negotiation period.

### 13.2 Arbitration

If good-faith negotiation does not resolve the dispute, either party may refer the matter to:
- The Uganda Centre for Arbitration and Dispute Resolution (UCADR) — for Uganda-based disputes
- The London Court of International Arbitration (LCIA) — for international disputes (as specified in the SPA)

Governing law: Ugandan law (unless otherwise specified in the SPA for international transactions). Proceedings conducted in English.

### 13.3 Preservation of Rights

Initiation of dispute resolution proceedings does not suspend either party's obligation to preserve documentation, samples, or shipment materials relevant to the dispute.

## 15. Fraud Prevention & Red Flag Policy

The gold trade in Africa is a high-fraud environment. DCA has a zero-tolerance policy for facilitation of fraud and maintains the following red-flag controls:

### 15.1 Common Fraud Patterns in the Gold Trade

Buyers and sellers are advised to be alert to:

| Red Flag | Description |
|---|---|
| Advance fee requests | Requests for upfront "fees", "taxes", or "insurance bonds" before goods are released |
| Unverifiable documentation | Licences or assay reports that cannot be independently verified |
| Pressure tactics | Artificial urgency to transact quickly without standard due diligence |
| Unrealistic pricing | Gold offered at more than 5–10% below spot price with no credible explanation |
| Offshore or unidentified intermediaries | Multiple layers of brokers with no clear principal |
| Requests to bypass banking | Requests for cash, cryptocurrency, or hawala payments |
| Inconsistent origin stories | Origin documentation that changes across communications |

### 15.2 DCA's Internal Controls

- No staff member is authorised to request or receive advance fees from buyers.
- All payment instructions are issued only on DCA letterhead by the compliance officer with dual authorisation.
- Buyers should verify any change of bank account details by telephone (using a number independently obtained, not from the email requesting the change) before making any payment.
- DCA will never request payment to a personal bank account.

### 15.3 Reporting Suspected Fraud

If you suspect you are the target of a fraud using DCA's name or documentation, contact:
- compliance@diamondcapitalafrica.com immediately
- Uganda Police Force — Serious Fraud Unit
- Interpol National Central Bureau (Uganda): +256 41 423 1796

DCA actively co-operates with law enforcement in fraud investigations.
