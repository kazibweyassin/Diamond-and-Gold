# Diamond Capital Africa — Website Improvement Recommendations

> Based on a full analysis of [diamondcapitalafrica.com](https://www.diamondcapitalafrica.com)  
> Prepared: May 2026

---

## 🔴 High Priority — Trust & Credibility

### 1. Add a Team Page
The single biggest trust gap. Named directors, account managers, and compliance officers with photos and LinkedIn profiles would dramatically increase credibility with institutional buyers. High-value gold transactions require knowing *who* you're dealing with.

### 2. Name Your Partners & Labs
"ISO Certified Labs" and "Assay Services" are anonymous. Replace these with actual institution names and logos (e.g. SGS, Bureau Veritas, Intertek). A named legal partner from McFord Advocates with a verifiable web presence would also help.

### 3. Add Verifiable Client References or Case Studies
Even 2–3 anonymized case studies ("a European refinery buyer, 50kg per quarter since 2022") with an offer to provide references on request would go a long way. The $2.8B trade volume claim needs something to anchor it.

### 4. Use Real Operational Photography
Replace the Unsplash/Pexels stock images in the field gallery with genuine on-site photos. Buyers who claim to "know the mines" should be able to show them — even if faces are blurred for privacy.

---

## 🟡 Medium Priority — Conversion & UX

### 5. Add a Formal Quote Request Form on the Homepage
The current flow relies heavily on WhatsApp or navigating to a contact page. A structured quote form (quantity, purity, destination, timeline) directly on the homepage would capture more serious buyer intent and feel more institutional.

### 6. Replace WhatsApp as the Primary CTA
Keep WhatsApp as an option (it's practical for your market), but make "Request a Formal Quote" or "Speak to a Specialist" the *primary* button. WhatsApp signals informality to European, Asian, and US institutional buyers.

### 7. Substantiate the Key Stats
"$2.8B+ Trade Volume" and "12+ Years Operating" need context. Add a footnote, a date range, or a brief explanation — e.g. *"cumulative value of facilitated transactions, 2013–2025."* Unanchored big numbers can actually reduce trust.

### 8. Add a News/Insights Section with Real Content
The News tab exists but should be regularly updated with relevant content — Uganda mining regulations, gold market commentary, OECD updates. This signals an active, knowledgeable operation and also improves SEO.

---

## 🟢 Lower Priority — Polish & Growth

### 9. Showcase the Investor Portal More Prominently
`invest.diamondcapitalafrica.com` is only mentioned in small footnotes. If it's a real product, it deserves its own dedicated section — the gold sector investment angle is a strong differentiator.

### 10. Add Live Chat (Not Just WhatsApp)
An Intercom or Tidio widget during business hours would let international buyers ask quick questions without committing to WhatsApp. Responses could still be handled by the same team.

### 11. Add the FAQ Page to Main Navigation
The FAQ exists (`/faq`) but isn't in the main navigation on the homepage. Institutional buyers have predictable questions — surfacing answers proactively reduces friction.

### 12. Consider Third-Party Review Integration
A Trustpilot profile or Google Business reviews widget, even with a modest number of reviews, adds an independently verifiable trust layer that no self-written copy can replicate.
